				<div class="paragraph" style="text-align:left;"><strong><font size="3">THE FRANCISCAN CREED</font></strong><br>Inspired by the vision of Saint Francis<br>we commit ourselves to the&nbsp;Gospel as our way&nbsp;of life. Our world view is centred&nbsp;in Christ<br><br>We see Jesus Christ as beginning&nbsp;the way and&nbsp;goal of all creation.&nbsp;This world-view reveals to us a God&nbsp;who&nbsp;is Father and a life which is&nbsp;Love.<br><br>This demands of us a life as&nbsp;brothers and sisters&nbsp;of all&nbsp;people&nbsp;<br>and of creation.&nbsp;We are on&nbsp;a pilgrimage&nbsp;trying to&nbsp;reach our Father,&nbsp;trying to live a life of&nbsp;Love.<br><br>We have not yet arrived;&nbsp;we are sinners,&nbsp;but we are called&nbsp;<br>to be Saints.&nbsp;As sinners,&nbsp;weak human beings,&nbsp;we must&nbsp;undergo&nbsp;a continuing conversion,&nbsp;returning always to&nbsp;the&nbsp;Father&nbsp;as prodigal sons and daughters.<br><br>Christ was poor and Christ was crucified;&nbsp;we seek to&nbsp;share&nbsp;in his poorness and in&nbsp;his suffering.&nbsp;We&nbsp;further&nbsp;commit ourselves to&nbsp;the service&nbsp;of the poor.&nbsp;<br><br>In making our way&nbsp;through life,&nbsp;we are guided more&nbsp;by&nbsp;simplicity,&nbsp;humility, and&nbsp;littleness&nbsp;than&nbsp;by power,&nbsp;<br>prestige and status.<br><br>Like Christ, like Francis, we seek to become<br>&nbsp;instruments of peace, peacemakers.&nbsp;Acknowledging the&nbsp;<br>guiding presence of the Holy Spirit,<br><br>We declare our loyalty to the Church&nbsp;in a spirit of dialogue&nbsp;<br>and cooperation with her ministers and shepherds.&nbsp;<br>We are&nbsp;conscious of&nbsp;our obligation to share in the<br>&nbsp;life and mission of the Church.<br><br>We are lay apostles called by Christ to continue his work&nbsp;<br>on earth. Our life and our spirituality, however&nbsp;is characteristically&nbsp;secular, in the world and for the world.<br><br>Joy sustains our lives, fulfills our living. &nbsp;We&nbsp;seek&nbsp;the same for others.&nbsp;To achieve all this is&nbsp;difficult;&nbsp;hence&nbsp;<br>we declare our need&nbsp;for Christ's&nbsp;Eucharist,&nbsp;<br>for personal, communal and liturgical prayer.<br></div>
